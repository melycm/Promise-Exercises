<p><b>Harry Edward Styles</b><sup id="cite_ref-Every_Piece_of_Me_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Every_Piece_of_Me-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born 1 February 1994) is an English singer, songwriter, and actor. He rose to stardom as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Boy_band" title="Boy band">boy band</a> <a href="/wiki/One_Direction" title="One Direction">One Direction</a>. Styles grew up in <a href="/wiki/Holmes_Chapel" title="Holmes Chapel">Holmes Chapel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, where he was the lead singer in a band. In 2010, Styles auditioned as a solo contestant for the British music competition series <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i>, where he and four other contestants were placed in a group to form One Direction. They have since released five albums, embarked on four <a href="/wiki/List_of_One_Direction_concert_tours" class="mw-redirect" title="List of One Direction concert tours">world tours</a>, and won <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_One_Direction" title="List of awards and nominations received by One Direction">several awards</a>, becoming one of most successful musical groups of all time.
</p><p>Five months after One Direction went on indefinite hiatus in January 2016, Styles signed a solo record deal with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>. His debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Sign_of_the_Times_(Harry_Styles_song)" title="Sign of the Times (Harry Styles song)">Sign of the Times</a>", was released in April 2017, reaching number one in the UK and number four in the US. Its music video earned him a <a href="/wiki/Brit_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Brit Award">Brit Award</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Harry_Styles_(album)" title="Harry Styles (album)">self-titled debut album</a> was released in May 2017, debuting atop the UK and US charts and received a generally positive critical reception. Styles made his film debut in <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s war film <i><a href="/wiki/Dunkirk_(2017_film)" title="Dunkirk (2017 film)">Dunkirk</a></i> (2017).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Harry Edward Styles was born in <a href="/wiki/Redditch" title="Redditch">Redditch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a> on 1 February 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is the son of Anne Cox (née Selley) and Desmond "Des" Styles, who worked in finance.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Many of his ancestors were farm labourers in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Styles was raised in <a href="/wiki/Holmes_Chapel" title="Holmes Chapel">Holmes Chapel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, after his parents moved there along with his older sister, Gemma,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> when he was a child.<sup id="cite_ref-Every_Piece_of_Me_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Every_Piece_of_Me-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Holmes_Chapel_Comprehensive_School" title="Holmes Chapel Comprehensive School">Holmes Chapel Comprehensive School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Styles's parents divorced when he was seven and his mother later was remarried to Robin Twist;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> he died in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Robin_Twist_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robin_Twist-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He has an older stepbrother named Mike and a stepsister named Amy, children of Twist.<sup id="cite_ref-Robin_Twist_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robin_Twist-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As a sixteen-year-old, he worked part-time at the W. Mandeville Bakery in Holmes Chapel.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a child, Styles loved singing, covering songs from <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> While at Holmes Chapel Comprehensive, Styles was the lead singer for the band White Eskimo, which won a local Battle of the Bands competition.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932015:_The_X_Factor_and_One_Direction"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2015:_The_X_Factor_and_One_Direction">2010–2015: <i>The X Factor</i> and One Direction</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg/220px-One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg/330px-One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg/440px-One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:One_Direction_X_Factor_Live_Glasgow.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Styles (left) with One Direction on <i>The X Factor</i> Live Tour in 2011</div></div></div>
<p>Following a suggestion from his mother, on 11 April 2010, Styles auditioned as a solo candidate for the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_7)" title="The X Factor (UK series 7)">seventh series</a> of the British televised singing competition <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK)">The X Factor</a></i>, singing a rendition of <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Isn%27t_She_Lovely" title="Isn&#39;t She Lovely">Isn't She Lovely</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Kelly_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelly-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He failed to progress to the "Boys" category at "<a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_7)#Judges&#39;_houses_and_wildcards" title="The X Factor (UK series 7)">judges' houses</a>". Four others in his age group also failed, but at the suggestion by <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a>, a guest judge,<sup id="cite_ref-Kelly_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelly-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> they were put together as an ensemble at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a> in July 2010, during the "<a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_7)#Bootcamp" title="The X Factor (UK series 7)">bootcamp</a>" stage of the competition,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> thus qualifying for the "Groups" category. The group consisting of Styles, <a href="/wiki/Niall_Horan" title="Niall Horan">Niall Horan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liam_Payne" title="Liam Payne">Liam Payne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Tomlinson" title="Louis Tomlinson">Louis Tomlinson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zayn_Malik" title="Zayn Malik">Zayn Malik</a>, got together for two weeks to get to know one another and practise.<sup id="cite_ref-thehothits1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thehothits1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated10_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated10-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Styles said he had suggested the name One Direction to his bandmates and they agreed to keep it.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For their qualifying song at "judges' houses", and their first song as a group, One Direction sang an acoustic version of "<a href="/wiki/Torn_(Ednaswap_song)" title="Torn (Ednaswap song)">Torn</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a> later commented that the performance convinced him that the group "were confident, fun, like a gang of friends, and kind of fearless as well."<sup id="cite_ref-simoncowellQ&amp;A_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simoncowellQ&amp;A-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Within the first four weeks of the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_7)#Live_show_details" title="The X Factor (UK series 7)">live shows</a>, they were Cowell's last act in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The group quickly gained popularity in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-simoncowellQ&amp;A_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simoncowellQ&amp;A-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>

Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Nilsson" title="Harry Nilsson">Harry Nilsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> as being influences.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Nilsson_88-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nilsson-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup> Styles praised Nilsson's lyrics as being "honest, and so good, and I think it’s because he’s never trying to sound clever.”<sup id="cite_ref-Nilsson_88-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nilsson-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup> Upon listening to Pink Floyd's 1973 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Side_of_the_Moon" title="The Dark Side of the Moon">The Dark Side of the Moon</a></i> as a child, he said he "couldn't really get it, but I just remember being like – this is really fucking cool."<sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His music contain elements of <a href="/wiki/Soft_rock" title="Soft rock">soft rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Britpop" title="Britpop">Britpop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a </p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:163px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HarryStyles2013-corte2.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/HarryStyles2013-corte2.png/161px-HarryStyles2013-corte2.png" width="161" height="236" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/HarryStyles2013-corte2.png/242px-HarryStyles2013-corte2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/HarryStyles2013-corte2.png/322px-HarryStyles2013-corte2.png 2x" data-file-width="1624" data-file-height="2376" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HarryStyles2013-corte2.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Styles in February 2013</div></div></div>
<p>Regarding Styles' time in One Direction, <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> writer Tim Sendra opined his "charming persona and elastic vocals had him positioned as the <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Timberlake</a> of the group."<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> Styles seemed "a dimpled, rakish prankster happy to wear the mantle of <a href="/wiki/Class_Clown" title="Class Clown">Class Clown</a>... maybe because he knew that <i>Most Likely to Succeed</i> belonged to him too," said <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Leah Greenblatt.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> During public appearances, Styles' fashion evolution has traded "teen-friendly" <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wills" title="Jack Wills">Jack Wills</a> tracksuits for a selection of <a href="/wiki/Yves_Saint_Laurent_(brand)" title="Yves Saint Laurent (brand)">Saint Laurent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci" title="Gucci">Gucci</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burberry" title="Burberry">Burberry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vogue_96-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As his time in the band progressed, the singer started wearing skinny jeans and boots.<sup id="cite_ref-vogue_96-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> For <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Chris Payne, "From his influences to his fashion to his onstage persona to his (formerly) flowing locks, Styles' vibe has always screamed rock star."<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> The same publication noted over the course of five-year stint with his group, Styles' taste in fashion "has blossomed from a teen wearing purple Jack Wills hoodies to a carefully executed blend of '70s rock with a glamorous magpie feel."<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> In September 2016, Styles appeared on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Another_Man" title="Another Man">Another Man</a></i> magazine, and was interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Handler" title="Chelsea Handler">Chelsea Handler</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup> prompting an editor from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> to label the lack of shirtless-ness and the focus on fashion for a niche publication as the death of a "heartthrob" and birth of an "artthrob".<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup> Promoting his solo projects during 2017, Styles opted for "candyfloss" pink suits, printed satin flares, gradually heightening heels, a style described as "flamboyant look."<sup id="cite_ref-vogue_96-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> Regarding his favouritism for pink, he quoted <a href="/wiki/The_Clash" title="The Clash">The Clash</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simonon" title="Paul Simonon">Paul Simonon</a> in an interview: "Pink is the only true rock &amp; roll colour."<sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kors" title="Michael Kors">Michael Kors</a>, “Harry is the modern embodiment of British rocker style - edgy, flamboyant and worn with unapologetic swagger."<sup id="cite_ref-50BestDressed2018_101-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50BestDressed2018-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He was ranked first and fifth in 2016 and 2017 respectively on <a href="/wiki/Vogue_(British_magazine)" title="Vogue (British magazine)">British <i>Vogue</i></a>’s "The 50 Fittest Boys".<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup> He was voted the "Sexiest Male in Pop" for 2016, 2017, and 2018 in a poll by British radio network <a href="/wiki/Capital_(radio_network)" title="Capital (radio network)">Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Styles was voted fourth by <a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">British <i>GQ</i></a> on their list of "50 best-dressed men".<sup id="cite_ref-50BestDressed2018_101-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50BestDressed2018-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Styles was in a relationship with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Xtra_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The Xtra Factor (UK TV series)">The Xtra Factor</a></i> presenter <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Flack" title="Caroline Flack">Caroline Flack</a> from November 2011 to January 2012, which caused controversy and criticism over their 14-year age gap.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup> Styles also dated American singer <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a> in late 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Styles has dozens of tattoos and had the words "<i>Late Late</i>" tattooed onto his arm during an appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_James_Corden" title="The Late Late Show with James Corden">The Late Late Show with James Corden</a></i> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Styles was named third on the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List" title="Sunday Times Rich List"><i>Sunday Times</i> Rich List</a> of musicians in the UK under 30.<sup id="cite_ref-networth_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-networth-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Styles has invested in the mindfulness app <a href="/wiki/Calm_(company)" title="Calm (company)">Calm</a>, which features videos, games and audio to help users relieve stress, founded by UK entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Michael_Acton_Smith" title="Michael Acton Smith">Michael Acton Smith</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">&#91;109&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:173px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harrycharityfootball1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Harrycharityfootball1.jpg/171px-Harrycharityfootball1.jpg" width="171" height="256" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Harrycharityfootball1.jpg/257px-Harrycharityfootball1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Harrycharityfootball1.jpg/342px-Harrycharityfootball1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1801" data-file-height="2701" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Harrycharityfootball1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Styles playing in The <a href="/wiki/Niall_Horan" title="Niall Horan">Niall Horan</a> Charity Football Challenge in 2014</div></div></div>
<p>In 2013, Styles along with bandmate <a href="/wiki/Liam_Payne" title="Liam Payne">Liam Payne</a> became ambassadors for the cancer charity Trekstock, and raised more than $800,000 through the online fundraising platform <a href="/wiki/Prizeo" title="Prizeo">Prizeo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Trekstock_110-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trekstock-110">&#91;110&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Do_Something_111-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Do_Something-111">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Styles sponsored water wells in India via Drop4Drop in support of <a href="/wiki/Lifewater_International" title="Lifewater International">Life Water'</a>s <a href="/wiki/World_Water_Day" title="World Water Day">World Water Day</a> campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">&#91;112&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Styles donated his hair to the UK charity <a href="/wiki/Little_Princess_Trust" title="Little Princess Trust">Little Princess Trust</a>, which supplies and funds high quality wigs made of real hair to children who have hair loss as a result of cancer treatment or other illnesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Bustle_113-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bustle-113">&#91;113&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, to celebrate the release of his debut album, Styles played intimate shows at <a href="/wiki/The_Garage,_London" title="The Garage, London">The Garage</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Troubadour_(West_Hollywood,_California)" title="Troubadour (West Hollywood, California)">Troubadour</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, with all proceeds benefiting charities.<sup id="cite_ref-UBP_114-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UBP-114">&#91;114&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_115-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-115">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Metro_116-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-116">&#91;116&#93;</a></sup> In October the same year, Styles alongside other artists performed at <a href="/wiki/CBS_Radio" title="CBS Radio">CBS Radio</a>’s 5th Annual We Can Survive concert at the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Bowl" title="Hollywood Bowl">Hollywood Bowl</a>, held in honour of National Breast Cancer Awareness Month with proceeds going to <a href="/wiki/Young_Survival_Coalition" title="Young Survival Coalition">Young Survival Coalition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup> By the end of his <a href="/wiki/Harry_Styles:_Live_on_Tour" title="Harry Styles: Live on Tour">first tour</a> in 2018, Styles had raised $1.2 million in charity donations from a portion of ticket and merchandise sales and <a href="/wiki/Live_Nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Live Nation">Live Nation</a>'s contributions for 62 charities around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> During the tour, Styles led a major effort in water conservation that saved upwards of 13,000 single-use water bottles, and recycled more than 6,500 gallons of water from buses, dressing rooms and other backstage areas.<sup id="cite_ref-119" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-119">&#91;119&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span></h3>
<p>Styles has supported various causes and social movements. He has been a long-standing advocate for equality and an ardent supporter of the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community and is frequently seen waving the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_flag_(LGBT_movement)" title="Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)">rainbow flag</a> on stage.<sup id="cite_ref-120" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-120">&#91;120&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, he released T-shirts with the slogan "Treat People With Kindness" in a rainbow print in celebration of <a href="/wiki/Pride_Month" class="mw-redirect" title="Pride Month">Pride Month</a>, with all proceeds benefiting <a href="/wiki/GLSEN" title="GLSEN">GLSEN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-121" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-121">&#91;121&#93;</a></sup> Styles has also advocated for racial and gender equality and stricter <a href="/wiki/Gun_control" title="Gun control">gun control</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-122" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-122">&#91;122&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-123" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-123">&#91;123&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Billboard2_124-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard2-124">&#91;124&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
